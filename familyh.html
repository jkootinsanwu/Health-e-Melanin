Family Practice

A study out of the University of Michigan shows that when physicians and patients share the same race or ethnicity, this improves time spent together, medication adherence, shared decision-making, wait times for treatment, cholesterol screening, patient understanding of cancer risk, and patient perceptions of treatment decisions (1). This means that patients trust their doctors more, and are more likely to listen to advice, including preventative care that generally improves the lifespan of patients.

Living longer means having the opportunity to make more lasting memories with loved ones - something we're sure that most people desire. Giving this opportunity to all people, regardless of skin color, would greatly improve the quality of life for many.
